# Local Business Marketing — Strategy Before Technology

## Summary

Growth playbook for local businesses (bakeries, salons, gyms, cafes) with strong word-of-mouth and limited marketing budget.

## Content

**Growth blocker diagnosis (ask first):** Is the problem discovery (people don't know you exist), conversion (they see you but don't visit), or retention (they don't return)?

**Phase 1 — Free channels (do before any website):**
- Google Business Profile: hours, photos, location, categories
- Review generation: ask happy customers after purchase
- Instagram/Facebook: product photos, behind-the-scenes, local hashtags
- Cost: $0–minimal

**Phase 2 — Local discovery:**
- Local SEO: "[business type] + [neighborhood]" keywords
- Directory listings (Yelp, local food/service guides)
- Community partnerships and local events

**Phase 3 — Website (only when justified):**
- Menu/catalog display, online ordering, inquiry forms, or booking
- When phone/WhatsApp volume is unmanageable or customers ask "do you have a website?"

**When Boolmind makes sense:** Phase 3+ when they need custom ordering, enrollment, payments, or integrated workflows — not as the first marketing step.

**Micro-budget honesty:** With ~$500/year marketing budget, recommend free channels only. Do not pitch custom development or Boolmind engagement yet.

**Pushback script:** For local businesses with 5km radius and strong referrals, a website is often NOT the highest-ROI first step. Google reviews and Instagram typically outperform a brochure website.
